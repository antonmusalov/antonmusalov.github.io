Project Summary

During my university days, I took on a unique project that involved gathering a large dataset of cow teat images. The project initially relied on a single thermal camera placed under the cow. While the approach showed promise, it had its limitationsâ€”especially when it came to uneven teats that led to some cows being misidentified. The results can be improved with further development.

To push the project further, I incorporated a D405 Intel 3D camera. This allowed for 3D identification of cow teats, a feature that could be leveraged for robotic cleaning and milking tasks in the future. However, a practical hurdle was the necessity for the camera to be directly beneath the cow, which doesn't quite align with real-world dairy shed conditions without some kind of lens-cleaning solution.

Throughout this project, I deepened my understanding of OpenCV algorithms and even dabbled in machine learning to try and identify cow teats from the images. While the results were inconclusive due to the limited dataset, the experience was invaluable in honing my skills in computer vision and machine learning.
