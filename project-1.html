Product Testing Station

During summer internship of 2021 I designed and built a product testing station to automate the production process.

>

Project Summary

Faced with the challenges of inconsistent and manual product testing, I developed an automated system to make the process more efficient. I set up a stationary jig armed with servo motors that triggered infrared sensors. This experience really sharpened my abilities in system automation, problem-solving, and standardizing procedures.

To better control the sequence of operations, I integrated a microcontroller with an optocoupler for reading 24V signals. This allowed us to monitor the spray sequence while also leaving room for operator-based visual inspections. The experience improved my grasp of system integration and control processes. I also implemented a Human-Machine Interface (HMI) that made it easier to input assembly numbers and test results. This really helped me get better at creating digital interfaces that people can actually use.

In the course of the project, I gained valuable experience in system setup and operation. I also got a deep dive into data logging protocols. The importance of network connectivity became clear, boosting my skills in both wireless and wired networking systems. Given the harsh workshop conditions, I also had to focus on waterproofing the entire setup.
