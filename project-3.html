Project Summary

The 3-Axis robot was specifically engineered for the accurate and efficient spraying of cow udders. Its electrical system features a Delta PLC, two 750W servo drives for the first two axes, and a pneumatic cylinder for the third, vertical axis. The system is versatile enough to run on both New Zealand's 230V single-phase and the USA's 480V power, all while sticking to AS/NZS 4024.1:2019 safety standards.

My time on this project deepened my appreciation for the role of industrial safety. Ensuring that the machine posed no risks to its environment or to those operating it became a focus point for me.

Additionally, I recognized the crucial role that detailed and accurate electrical drawings play. They were invaluable not just for assembly and troubleshooting, but also for the seamless integration of various hardware components, such as the Delta PLC and servo drives.

This emphasis on safety and electrical best practices highlighted the need for thorough planning and a commitment to international standards. These insights have convinced me that such an approach is vital for ensuring the machine's longevity and reliability in different industrial settings.
