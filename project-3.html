Project Summary

The 3-Axis robot was designed for precise and efficient spraying of cow udders. The electrical system integrates a Delta PLC, two 750W servo drives for the first two axes, and a pneumatically driven cylinder for the third vertical axis. The design operates on both NZ 230V single-phase and USA 480V using a transformer, adhering to AS/NZS 4024.1:2019 safety standards.

During my involvement with the 3-Axis robot project, I gained a profound understanding of the significance of industrial safety. Ensuring the machine operates without posing risks to its immediate environment or users became a paramount concern. In addition, I came to appreciate the necessity of comprehensive and accurate electrical drawings. These drawings not only facilitate smoother assembly and troubleshooting but also ensure the seamless integration of various components, like the Delta PLC and the servo drives. The emphasis on safety, combined with these electrical design practices, highlighted the importance of meticulous planning and strict adherence to international standards, ensuring the longevity and reliability of the machine in varied industrial settings.
